Animated Documentary: The Siege of Leningrad

Documentary motion project created to convey the emotional weight and historical context of the Siege of Leningrad through animation, rhythm and restrained visual language.

  • Role: Motion Designer
  • Tools: After Effects, Illustrator, Photoshop
  • Year: 2024
  • Type: Personal / Documentary project
  • Recognition: Winner of the All-Russian animation competition “Animir” — Documentary Animation

Context

This project was created as a documentary animation focused on one of the most tragic periods in history — the Siege of Leningrad.

Goal

The goal was not to recreate historical events literally, but to communicate the emotional atmosphere, human experience and sense of time through motion, pacing and visual restraint.

Concept & Approach

I approached the project with a minimal and restrained visual style, avoiding decorative animation in favor of clarity, pauses and rhythm.

Motion was used as a narrative tool — not to entertain, but to support the story and emotional tension.

Motion Design Focus

Special attention was paid to timing, silence and transitions between scenes. Slower pacing and controlled motion were intentionally used to reflect the weight of the subject and allow the viewer to absorb the content.

Process

The process included research, storyboard development and multiple motion tests to find the right balance between expressiveness and restraint.

All animation was created in After Effects, with a focus on clean transitions and a consistent visual language.

Result & Recognition

The final animation delivers a clear and emotionally grounded narrative within a short time frame.

The project was awarded first place in the Animir All-Russian animation competition in the Documentary Animation category.
