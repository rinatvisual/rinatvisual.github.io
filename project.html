Animated Documentary: The Siege of Leningrad

Documentary motion project created to convey the emotional weight and historical context of the Siege of Leningrad through animation, rhythm and restrained visual language.

  • Role: Motion Designer
  • Tools: After Effects, Illustrator, Photoshop
  • Year: 2024
  • Type: Personal / Documentary project
  • Recognition: Winner of the Animir All-Russian animation competition — Documentary Animation

Goal

The goal was not to recreate historical events literally, but to communicate emotional atmosphere, human experience and a sense of time through motion and pacing.

Concept & Approach

The project uses a restrained visual style where motion serves the narrative. Pauses, silence and rhythm play a central role in conveying meaning.

Breakdown

Narrative & Rhythm

Slow pacing and pauses are used to let the viewer feel the weight of the story.

Visual Language

Minimal color palette and reduced graphic elements keep focus on atmosphere.

Motion & Transitions

Transitions guide attention without breaking emotional continuity.

Timing & Silence

Moments of stillness reinforce the documentary tone.

Result & Recognition

The project was awarded first place at the Animir All-Russian animation competition in the Documentary Animation category.
